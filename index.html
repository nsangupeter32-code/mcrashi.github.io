Our Story

Founded on one truth: a hungry child cannot learn. MCRSAHI exists to break that cycle — bringing organic agriculture, clean nutrition, and climate resilience to rural schools in Western Province.

Youth-Led & Community-Rooted

Every project, every school, every harvest — driven by young people in Western Province.

Who We Are

A Youth-Led
Movement

We are a non-governmental, non-profit organisation dedicated to connecting rural schools in Western Province with climate-smart agriculture, nutrition programmes, and community health solutions.

🌱 Transparency 💡 Innovation ⚖️ Equity 🌍 Sustainability 🤝 Partnership 🌿 Env. Responsibility
Mission

To connect vulnerable rural schools with funding and support for school-based climate-smart and organic agriculture projects in Western Province, improving education outcomes, health, and community resilience.

What We Do

Our Four
Thematic Pillars

🌦️

Climate Change & Schools

Promoting resilience through school gardens, water harvesting systems, renewable solutions, and sustainable land use across Western Province.

🥦

Health & Nutrition

Addressing malnutrition through school feeding support, nutrition-sensitive agriculture, and education on healthy, locally-grown diets.

🌱

Smart Agriculture for Youth

Empowering pupils with hands-on farming, composting, mulching, natural pest control, conservation farming, and agroforestry.

🌍

Organic & Regenerative Agriculture

Restoring soil, protecting ecosystems, reducing synthetic chemicals, and teaching pupils to grow healthy food in harmony with nature.

Strategic Direction

Our Core
Objectives

01Establish and support school-based climate-smart agriculture projects in Western Province.
02Improve nutrition and health outcomes among pupils through school gardens and feeding initiatives.
03Promote organic and chemical-free farming practices across rural school communities.
04Build practical agricultural and life skills among pupils and youth.
05Strengthen resilience of schools and surrounding communities to climate change.
06Facilitate access to funding, tools, and training for school-led initiatives.
07Integrate agricultural learning with school curricula to advance sustainability education.
08Support the use of locally available organic inputs such as compost and manure.
09Identify and support innovative ideas from pupils, teachers, and local community members.
